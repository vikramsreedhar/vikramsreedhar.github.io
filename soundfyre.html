Dropping a "hot" mixtape

My friend and I decided to go to CalHacks 2.0 in October. I had a few friends at Cal, so I called them up to see if they would be participating at the hackathon too. Luckily, they were and they offered sleeping arrangements in their apartment for me and my friend which was really awesome of them.

Once we arrived at Cal, we met up with my friends who were letting us crash in their apartment and we got started on the ideation process right away. We had no idea what we wanted to hack on, but we all agreed we wanted to do something related to music. That's when I came up with the idea SoundFyre. What we created, was a music location service website that allows underground artists to upload their music onto a Google Map and see how "hot" their song is doing locally and globally. I'm very passionate about this project because my best friend in high school is an underground musician and wants to make it big one day. However, it is a hard career path so I wanted to make something that would benefit him.

We presented our project to judges and other participants and we got a lot of great feedback and support! We really didn't expect people to like our project because we didn't intend on winning any prizes, we just created it for fun. However, once we saw how much buzz it was getting, we decided to turn this idea into a possible start-up. A CEO reached out to us and told us that if we made major updates and improvements to the site and maybe even create an app for it, he would be interested in funding us. I definitely learned that if you're passionate about a project or idea, you can definitely turn it into something big and spectacular.
