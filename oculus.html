The Beginning of a New Reality

Sword Art Online. This was THE coolest anime I had ever seen and I couldn't be more excited about the future it described. In the anime, almost everyone had a virtual reality headsets, which allowed one to enter a virtual world where they could become a warrior, knight, or even sorcerer. Once in this new world, you could talk with other players, explore the wilderness, fly, or do anything that you could imagine. After finishing this anime, I knew exactly what field of computer science I wanted to get involved with.

The biggest virtual reality manufacturer currently is Oculus, and I knew that I had to get my hands on an Oculus Rift, the current virtual reality headset out right now, as soon as possible. However, they are quite expensive, so I quickly realized that my dream of exploring virtual reality may take a lot longer than I expected.

During a meeting with my research team at Stanford over the summer, my professor described his trip to Korea to meet with Samsung Executives. He then told us about how he got to try out an Oculus headset and was very impressed by the quality. He expressed a lot of interest in virtual reality and wanted to propose a project in this area. I immediately jumped out of my seat and said I would lead the project. I knew that this was my chance to begin work in the virutal reality field and I had to grab at this opportunity now or never. My professor was pleased to see my excitement and said he could buy me an Oculus Rift to work on for the project. Sword Art Online seemed ever more real at this moment and I'm glad that I didn't miss an opportunity like this.
