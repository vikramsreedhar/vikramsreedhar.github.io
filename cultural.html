Once Zapata A Time

My friend Veronica bust into my room and asked me if I had applied to volunteer for New Student Orientation(NSO) at Stanford yet. I told her I was planning to, but hadn't done so yet. She said I should apply for the Community Orientation Volunteer position for the Chicano/Latino community instead of just a regular volunteer position because she was the leader for that team. I was unsure about joining her team because I didn't speak Spanish and didn't know anything about this community. However, she reassured me that none of that mattered and this would be a cool experience for me. I gave in and decided I would join her team.

I met my team and the community leaders soon after and became very close with all of them. The faculty members for the Chicano/Latino community were very caring and welcoming and immediately accepted me into their community family with no hesitation. Even though I couldn't speak and understand Spanish, my group members would translate for me during talks so that I could follow along as well. Overall, I felt at home in this community and was glad that I got to experience a different side of Stanford for a week.
